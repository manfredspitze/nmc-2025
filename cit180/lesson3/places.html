A Few Definitions

Each synopsis below uses the following terms:

Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
The total value of all goods and services a country produces.
GDP is a common indicator of a country's economic health.
Per Capita
This means “per person”.
Real GDP
Gross Domestic Product (GDP) that is adjusted for inflation.
Inflation refers to the increase in prices over time.
Note that if a country's GDP increases, but prices are also increasing, the actual increase in production might not be as large as you might expect initially.
Real GDP takes this situation into account, and so provides a more accurate picture of a country's actual economic growth.
Real GPD per capita allows you to compare the economic well-being of people in different countries or track the growth of a country's economy over time in a way that takes price fluctuations into account.
